    Purpose of Case Studies

    A case study is a rather broad concept that varies depending on the subject or the focus area. First of all, it’s defined as an empirical investigation into a phenomenon, event, period, decision, policy, institution, persona or group that is performed in a real-life context. It also designates the research strategy used for such studies. Case studies typically include extensive research and an analysis of both qualitative and quantitative data, often generated through experiments performed by their authors.

    The purpose of case studies is normally to derive valuable judgments that allow a deeper understanding of the topic. To do this, they might focus on certain traits of interest especially when exploring individuals, groups, on behavioral patterns, causes, or underlying principles/ laws that govern certain events or phenomena. They may often start with theoretical propositions/hypotheses that are explored in-depth and proven with evidence, either from literature or experimental.

    Subjects That We Can Work With

    The format followed by case studies varies greatly depending on the subject – in sciences like psychology, for instance, case studies might involve following a patient’s history and the effect of treatment approaches. Below are only a few examples of disciplines and subjects for which case study help might be requested:

    • Business and management like analyzing stocks or supply chains, acquisitions, the advent of innovative or exemplary businesses, asset management in big companies, the efficiency of certain corporate responsibility practices. Case studies in business might investigate specific decisions, events, periods, or market entities, taking into account circumstances, market evolution, descriptive economic parameters.
    • Marketing - analyzing traditional or innovative advertising campaigns, particularly effective strategies used in social media, adapting advertising messages to multi-cultural, cosmopolitan audiences, the importance of SEO for online industries.
    • Social sciences - case studies relating to anthropology, history, geography, psychology, political science, sociology and social studies.
    • Nursing and medical disciplines. In medical sciences, a case study investigates pathologies along with causes, mechanisms, and treatments.
    • Life science subjects - ecology, cell biology, developmental biology, genetics, evolutionary biology, immunology, neuroscience, molecular biology, microbiology, histology, physiology, pharmacology.
